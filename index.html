5. Identify & Document Control Exceptions

Findings
Task 5: Identify & Document Control Exceptions

Background

An exception occurs when a control does not operate as intended. Each exception must be formally documented as a finding with full context using the 5Cs framework.

Finding Structure (5Cs)

Criteria: What should be happening (policy / standard / regulation)
Condition: What you actually observed during testing
Cause: Root cause of the gap
Consequence (Effect): Risk or impact to NovaBridge
Corrective Action (Recommendation): Your suggested remediation

Internal Audit Charter — NovaBridge Financial Services

1. Purpose & Authority

The Internal Audit Department (IAD) of NovaBridge Financial Services is established by authority of the Board of Directors and the Audit Committee. The IAD operates independently of management and has full, free, and unrestricted access to all functions, records, property, and personnel of NovaBridge.

2. Mission

To provide independent, objective assurance and consulting activity designed to add value and improve NovaBridge's operations, risk management, internal controls, and governance processes.

3. Scope of IT Audit

IT audit activities encompass: information security, access management, change management, IT general controls (ITGC), cloud infrastructure, third-party/vendor risk, business continuity and disaster recovery, data privacy, and regulatory compliance (SOC 2 Type II, SOX ITGC, OCC examination readiness).

4. Independence & Objectivity

The Chief Audit Executive (CAE) reports functionally to the Audit Committee and administratively to the CEO. No IT auditor shall audit activities for which they have had management responsibility in the prior 12 months. All impairments to independence must be disclosed immediately to the CAE.

5. Standards

The IAD adheres to the IIA International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing, ISACA CISA standards, and applicable regulatory guidance from the OCC and FFIEC.

Audit Department Code of Conduct

1. Independence & Objectivity

All IT auditors must maintain independence in fact and appearance. You may not audit any area where you have had management responsibility, a financial interest, or a personal relationship with the auditee within the past 12 months. All impairments must be disclosed immediately to your manager.

2. Confidentiality

All information obtained during audit engagements is strictly confidential. Audit findings, workpapers, and evidence may not be shared outside the Internal Audit Department without written CAE authorization. This obligation continues after employment ends.

3. Professional Conduct

Auditors must treat all auditees with professionalism and respect. Findings must be supported by sufficient, reliable evidence. Auditors must never exaggerate or minimize findings and must present facts objectively.

4. Conflicts of Interest

Any personal, financial, or professional relationship that could impair — or appear to impair — independence must be disclosed annually and upon discovery. This includes financial holdings in audited vendors or subsidiaries.

5. Reporting Violations

Violations of this Code must be reported to the Audit Manager or CAE. NovaBridge prohibits retaliation. Anonymous reporting: Ethics Hotline 1-800-NB-ETHIC.
